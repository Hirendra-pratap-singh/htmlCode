What the prime minister said

NEW DELHI : Prime Minister Narendra Modi on Wednesday asserted that instances of violence that have roiled Manipur for over a year have begun to decline, and that the resumption of schools, offices and establishments in the state is a sign of normalcy being restored, his first comments in Parliament on the issue. Pointing out that the President’s Rule was imposed 10 times in the state during the Congress rule, the PM, during his speech in the Rajya Sabha on Wednesday, cautioned that anyone trying to “fan the fire” by stoking unrest in the state should stop as the “state will reject them”. The opposition staged a walkout barely 30 minutes after the Prime Minister began his address after leader of opposition in the house Mallikarjun Kharge was not allowed to intervene.
